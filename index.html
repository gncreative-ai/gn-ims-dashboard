Nifty price with dynamic walls & overlays

Nifty price candles (teal = up interval, red = down). Call wall (dashed red) marks the strike carrying the heaviest call open interest — it often acts as resistance; put wall (dotted green) marks the heaviest put OI — it often acts as support. The amber line is the ZLEMA trigger; the grey dashed band is the VPC channel (context, shown when its checkbox below is on). Triangle markers show confirmed signal-engine triggers, labelled with the gate score. When Magnet Snap is active, a red/green dashed line pair shows the live stop-loss and target while a position is open — both disappear together the moment either is hit. Toggle extra strike overlays and the expected-move band from the Overlays row above.

ZLEMA/VPC drive the Setup signal panel below (both checked = confluence, AND not either/or). Magnet Snap is a standalone strategy — checking it ignores ZLEMA/VPC entirely.

Rolling ATM call and put premium

Live premium of the at-the-money call and put. Both falling = volatility bleeding out of the options; both rising = volatility expanding. A circle marks each point where the ATM strike rolled to a new level as spot moved.

Rate of change of total call open interest each 3-minute interval. Blue bars = calls being added (potential resistance building); red bars = calls being unwound.

Put OI velocity

Building (blue)Unwinding (red)

Rate of change of total put open interest each 3-minute interval. Blue bars = puts being added (potential support building); red bars = puts being unwound.

Net OI velocity, call minus put

Calls building faster (blue)Puts building faster (red)

Call OI velocity minus put OI velocity. Positive (blue) = calls being stacked faster than puts; negative (red) = puts being stacked faster. A quick read on which side is getting loaded.

Setup signal — live

Client-side signal engine: a ZLEMA cross and/or a VPC (volume-free) breakout on the 3-minute candles, held for a confirmation bar, then scored against the options chain (max pain, walls, GEX, PCR). Shows the most recent confirmed trigger that passed the gate.

Stop loss and target are structural levels (channel bounds, walls, max pain) — not risk-managed position sizing. Confirm position size independently.

Setup signal — history

Every confirmed trigger in the loaded range, most recent first — whether or not it passed the options gate.
